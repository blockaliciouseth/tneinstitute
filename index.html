Pathway Agreements

Articulation Agreements

Start your degree at a local African university , then transfer to a top global institution to complete it. Lower overall cost, local start, world-class finish. Some agreements award certificates from both institutions.

What is an articulation agreement? A formal agreement between two universities that allows credit from one to be recognised at the other. A student might complete 1, 2, or 3 years locally, then transfer abroad , paying lower local fees for the early years and only moving internationally for the final stage. The receiving university awards the degree; in some cases both institutions award certificates.

Guide for students

What is Transnational Education?

TNE , Transnational Education , means studying for a degree from a university in a different country to the one awarding it. Earn a UK, Australian, Canadian or US degree without leaving your home region. For a comprehensive independent guide to TNE quality and standards, visit tneinstitute.com →

Why does TNE matter?

Instead of spending £25,000+ per year studying in the UK, you might study the same University of Birmingham degree in Dubai for significantly less , with lower living costs, no UK visa complexity, and a much shorter flight from home. The degree awarded is identical. Employers recognise the awarding institution , not where you physically studied.

Important: Always verify that the TNE campus is accredited by the national body in the delivery country and that the degree is formally awarded by the home university. TNE Institute only lists programmes where both conditions are confirmed. For detailed guidance on TNE quality frameworks, visit tneinstitute.com.
The four main TNE delivery models
🏛
Branch Campus
A university opens a full physical campus in another country under its own brand. The parent university controls curriculum and awards the degree.
e.g. University of Birmingham Dubai, CMU Africa (Kigali)
🤝
Franchise Programme
A local institution delivers a programme designed by an overseas university, which awards the degree and maintains quality control.
e.g. A local college delivering a University of Hertfordshire programme
🔗
Joint / Dual Degree
Two universities collaborate on delivery. Students may receive degrees from one or both institutions on completion.
e.g. Joint MSc awarded by a UK and East African university
💻
Online / Distance
Full programme delivered online. Study from anywhere. The degree carries the same weight as an on-campus award.
e.g. University of London Worldwide BSc
How is quality assured?
🏆
Same Degree Award
The certificate is issued by the home university. Non-negotiable for reputable TNE.
📋
Curriculum Control
The home university controls curriculum, assessments and marking throughout.
🔍
External Review
UK programmes reviewed by the QAA. Australian by TEQSA. Regular mandatory inspections.
🌍
British Council Register
Public register of UK TNE activity , a primary verification source used by TNE Institute.
📊
Employer Recognition
Major employers evaluate the awarding institution, not delivery location. A Birmingham degree is a Birmingham degree.
⚠️
What to Watch For
Avoid programmes where the awarding institution is unclear or where the local partner issues the degree.

Our Data

TNE Institute aggregates data from multiple public and licensed sources. This page explains where each data type comes from, how current it is, and what you must independently verify.

Understanding the search filters
🏆
What is QS World Ranking?
QS World University Rankings ranks universities globally across 6 indicators including academic reputation, employer reputation, faculty-to-student ratio, and research impact. A university ranked #1–100 is in the global top 1% , exceptionally competitive and internationally recognised. TNE Institute only includes universities within the top 1000. Learn more at topuniversities.com.
📚
What is QS Subject Ranking?
QS also ranks universities by subject area , so a university ranked #300 overall might be in the global top 50 for Engineering or Business. Subject rankings are often more relevant than overall rank when choosing a specific degree. A university in the QS top 100 for your subject is genuinely world-class in that field, regardless of its overall position.
🎓
What is UG vs PGT?
UG (Undergraduate) , your first degree, typically studied after secondary school (A-Levels, IB, or equivalent). Usually 3–4 years. You graduate with a Bachelor's degree (BSc, BA, BEng, LLB etc.).

PGT (Postgraduate Taught) , a Masters-level programme, typically 1–2 years, studied after completing a Bachelor's degree. You graduate with an MSc, MA, MBA, LLM etc.
🌍
Why only these 7 disciplines?
Business, Accountancy, Engineering, Computer Science/AI, Law, Medicine and Economics represent the most popular disciplines among international students globally , and the ones with the strongest overseas campus and articulation provision. Focusing here allows us to maintain data quality. We plan to expand to additional disciplines as TNE Institute grows.
🏛
What is TNE / an overseas campus?
Transnational Education (TNE) means studying for a degree from a university in a different country to the one that awards it. A branch campus is a full university campus opened in another country , like University of Birmingham Dubai. The degree awarded is identical to the home campus. For a comprehensive guide to TNE quality standards, visit tneinstitute.com.

⚡ The Amazon Moment for Education
Amazon didn't just make shopping cheaper. It fundamentally transferred power from retailers to consumers , giving people access to more choice, more information, and more value than ever before, without leaving home.

The same shift is happening in higher education right now. A student in Lagos, Nairobi, Accra or Port Louis can today access a University of Birmingham degree in Dubai, a Carnegie Mellon degree in Kigali, or a London School of Economics degree online without the extortionate UK living costs, the visa lottery, or the 12-hour flight. The degree on their CV is identical. The cost is dramatically lower.

Most students don't know this yet. TNE Institute exists to change that.
Students are now in charge

For decades, the international higher education market worked on one model: students paid whatever universities asked, moved wherever universities were located, and navigated opaque information systems designed to serve institutions, not learners.

That model is breaking down. Students now have the voice, the choice, and , critically , they hold the financial power. Universities that want to grow internationally must come to students, not the other way around. The explosion of overseas campuses, online degrees, and transnational partnerships is direct evidence of this shift.

Which campus is right for you?
🏆
If you want to start now
Only Southampton is currently open. If you are applying for August 2025 or August 2026 entry, Southampton is your only confirmed option. Liverpool is the next most advanced, with applications open for 2026.
🏅
If Russell Group is essential
Southampton, Liverpool, Bristol, and Queen's Belfast are all Russell Group. York, Aberdeen, Lancaster, Surrey, and Coventry are not. For most Indian employers this matters less than subject and class of degree, but it remains significant for UK employers.
📊
If ranking is the priority
Bristol (#51) is highest-ranked, followed by Southampton (#81), Liverpool (#147), York (#169), Lancaster (#209), Queen's Belfast (#253), Surrey (#323), Aberdeen (#383). Bristol costs the most; Liverpool costs the least among the top four.
💰
If cost is the priority
Liverpool at ₹11.5L/yr is the most affordable confirmed Russell Group option. York at ₹12.5L/yr is similar without Russell Group status. Bristol at ₹15–17L/yr is the most expensive among those with confirmed fees.
🔄
If UK transfer matters to you
Liverpool stands out: the final year in Liverpool is included in India tuition fees — genuinely integrated, not an optional add-on. All others offer semester exchanges or year abroad options as separate arrangements.
💻
If you want tech or AI
Liverpool (CS, Software Development, Game Design) for Bengaluru's tech ecosystem. York (CS with AI & Cyber Security, Creative Industries) for specialists. Bristol (Data Science, Economics & Data Science) for quant roles. Surrey (AI, Cyber Security, Finance) in GIFT City.
⚠️ A note on timing: All fees and course lists are based on the latest confirmed information as of April 2026. For campuses not yet open, details are subject to change.
